### 🔵 MDF Governance Tracker
**File:** `mdf-governance.html`

**What it is:**
An end-to-end operating system for managing MDF (Market Development Fund) claims across vendors, regions, and close cycles. Tracks every claim from commitment through proof-of-performance submission and reimbursement — surfacing blocked items, ownership gaps, accrual readiness, and exception escalations before they become month-end problems.

**What it covers:**
- Top Actions Today — prioritized by urgency, owner, and region
- MDF Claims Control Table — claim status (Blocked / In Review / Cleared) per vendor across EMEA, APAC, Americas, LATAM
- Accrual Readiness scoring — close-support quality rated per program
- Reimbursement Aging — submitted claims tracked by age and review status
- Claim Owner Accountability — individual-level ownership with escalation flags
- Proof of Performance checklists — completion tracking per support package
- Exception Escalation queue — named blockers with next-action deadlines
- Operating SOPs — MDF checklist, accrual support, reimbursement template, follow-up cadence, cutoff systems, escalation guidance

**Who it's for:**
VP of Finance, CMO, or Head of Channel / Partner Marketing who needs to know: *Which claims are at risk this close cycle? Who is blocking reimbursement? What is accrual-ready today?*

**Why it exists:**
MDF programs at scale — multiple vendors, multiple regions, multiple proof requirements — break down without an operating system. This module shows how to govern that complexity in a way finance and marketing can both use.

**Real-life scenario:**
> Month-end is in 3 days. Fourteen active claims. Three are Blocked waiting on proof of performance. Two are in reimbursement review with no follow-up logged. The accrual for Q2 is incomplete. This module shows exactly who owns what, what is missing, and the fastest path to close.

**Long-term path alignment:**

| Stage | How this module serves it |
|---|---|
| **Commercial / GTM Finance Manager** | Direct proof of marketing spend governance at scale — vendor accountability, accrual discipline, exception escalation operating rhythm |
| **Strategic Finance / FP&A Senior Manager** | Demonstrates ability to build and own a finance operating system across regions and stakeholders, not just models |
| **Director / VP Finance** | Shows operating rhythm design and cross-functional accountability infrastructure — the kind of system a finance leader builds and delegates |
| **CFO / Head of Finance** | Proof that you design governance systems that reduce close risk, improve reimbursement recovery, and give leadership a clean decision view |

### 🔵 Operations Finance & Spend Analytics
**File:** `operations-spend.html`

**What it is:**
A spend control center for operating finance — tracks vendor rate discipline, accrual completeness, forecast vs. actual variance, and exception flags across a multi-vendor, multi-budget-owner environment. Runs in three randomized outcome modes: **Positive (clean run) / Mid (watch spend) / Negative (leakage alert)** — each generating a coherent scenario end-to-end.

**What it covers:**
- Vendor Rate Cards & Rate Discipline — benchmark vs. contracted rate by vendor, with leakage identification and top vendors ranked by savings opportunity
- Accrual Tracking — incurred-not-yet-invoiced tracking with accrual completion rate and aging
- Forecast vs. Actual Spend — monthly variance analysis with budget/forecast/actual breakdown by period
- Exception Queue & Actionable Flags — color-coded exception cards (red/orange/green) with recommended next actions per item

**Who it's for:**
VP of Finance, Head of Operations Finance, or Finance Business Partner who needs to know: *Where is spend leaking? What is not yet invoiced? Where is forecast accuracy breaking down? What needs action today?*

**Why it exists:**
Finance teams that only review spend after close are always reacting late. This module shows how to build a forward-looking spend control system that surfaces problems while there is still time to act.

**Real-life scenario:**
> OpEx spiked vs. budget and nobody saw it coming. Three vendors are billing above contracted rate. Accrual completeness is at 89% with 4 days until cut-off. This module surfaces the gap, ranks the leakage, and flags the next action for each open item.

**Long-term path alignment:**

| Stage | How this module serves it |
|---|---|
| **Commercial / GTM Finance Manager** | Core proof of marketing and GTM spend governance — vendor rate discipline, accrual rigor, exception management at scale |
| **Strategic Finance / FP&A Senior Manager** | Demonstrates forecast-vs-actual operating discipline and ability to manage spend governance across a large budget owner population |
| **Director / VP Finance** | Shows design of a spend control operating system — not just variance analysis, but structured exception management and escalation logic |
| **CFO / Head of Finance** | Proof of financial control infrastructure — accrual completeness, rate discipline, and exception queues that reduce close surprises |

### 🔵 Commercial & Sales Finance
**File:** `commercial-sales.html`

**What it is:**
A commercial decision support model for marketing and sales investment decisions — evaluating promotional ROI, channel P&L performance, price/volume/mix variance, and trade discount accrual schedules. Designed to answer the questions a CFO or VP of Revenue asks before approving spend.

**What it covers:**
- Promotion ROI Analysis — before/after comparison of COGS, revenue, gross margin, and return on ad spend for a given campaign
- Channel-level P&L — 12-month revenue performance by channel with budget vs. actual and variance flags
- Price / Volume / Mix Bridge — decomposed revenue variance showing how much of the change came from price decisions vs. volume vs. mix shift
- Trade Discount & Accrual Schedule — straight-line accrual logic for trade spend commitments with reforecast and revenue reconciliation

**Who it's for:**
CFO, VP of Sales, VP of Marketing, or Commercial Finance Manager who needs to know: *Did the promo make money? Which channel is underperforming and why? Is the revenue variance a price problem or a volume problem?*

**Why it exists:**
Commercial decisions made without financial modeling either over-invest in low-return programs or kill high-potential initiatives based on gut feel. This module forces the math on promotional and channel decisions before commitment.

**Real-life scenario:**
> Sales wants to run a promotion. Marketing says the last one worked. Finance has no model to test the claim. This module runs the before/after ROI comparison, shows the gross margin impact, and gives a clear go / no-go verdict with the numbers behind it.

### 🔵 Revenue Bridge & Cohort Retention
**File:** `revenue-bridge.html`

**What it is:**
A revenue composition and retention analysis model that explains why revenue changed — decomposing movement into new customer adds, expansion, churn, and price — and whether growth quality is healthy or acquisition-dependent.

**What it covers:**
- Revenue Bridge (Month 1 → Month 12) — waterfall chart showing each revenue driver: starting base, new customers, expansion, churn, price change, ending revenue
- NRR / GRR Board View — Net Revenue Retention, Gross Revenue Retention, churn rate, expansion rate, and growth quality signal (Healthy / Leaking / At Risk)
- 12-Month Revenue Trend — stacked bar chart separating base revenue, new, expansion, and churn lost by month
- Cohort Retention Table — 6 cohorts tracked across 11 time periods with color-coded retention health (green / amber / red)

**Who it's for:**
CFO, VP of Revenue, or Head of Customer Success who needs to know: *Is growth durable or dependent on new customer volume? Where is churn compounding? Which cohorts are deteriorating?*

**Why it exists:**
Revenue growth looks the same on a top-line chart whether it is healthy expansion or a leaky bucket filled faster than it drains. This module shows the composition — and gives a clear verdict on whether retention is strong enough to scale on.

**Real-life scenario:**
> Revenue grew 107% year-over-year. The board is pleased. But NRR is 98% — meaning the existing base is barely retaining. Every dollar of growth came from new customer adds. If acquisition slows, revenue falls. This module shows that story before the board asks.

### 🔵 SaaS / Fintech Unit Economics
**File:** `unit-economics.html`

**What it is:**
A unit economics decision model that evaluates the financial health and scalability of a customer acquisition and retention model — from CAC and LTV through contribution margin, run rate projection, and cash & runway impact.

**What it covers:**
- CAC / LTV Engine — customer acquisition cost, lifetime value, LTV:CAC ratio, payback period, and a 0–100 unit economics health score
- Capital Allocation — growth investment scenario with cost per acquired customer, total capital requirement, and projected revenue return
- Working Capital Impact — how growth scenarios affect cash flow timing at different acquisition rates
- Contribution Margin Analysis — monthly contribution by cohort, blended margin trend, and margin per customer at scale
- Run Rate Forecasting — revenue projections across Base / Upside / Downside scenarios with growth rate assumptions
- Cash & Runway Impact — how the unit economics model flows into a cash and runway view
- Integration into Enterprise Financial Model — how unit economics connects to P&L, balance sheet, and cash flow assumptions

**Who it's for:**
CFO, VP of Finance, or Head of Growth who needs to know: *Is this acquisition model profitable at scale? At what volume does contribution margin turn positive? What does growth cost — in cash — at our current CAC?*

**Why it exists:**
Growth teams optimize for volume. Finance needs to know if volume is creating value or destroying it. This module makes unit economics legible to non-finance executives and gives finance a structured framework for governing growth investment.

**Real-life scenario:**
> Growth team wants to scale paid acquisition. CAC has been rising for 3 quarters. Leadership is split on whether to invest or pause. This module shows the LTV:CAC trend, the payback at current CAC, what contribution margin looks like at 2x volume, and how much cash the growth plan requires.

### 🔵 KPI Tree & Driver Sensitivity
**File:** `kpi-tree-sensitivity.html`

**What it is:**
A driver decomposition model that maps a business's KPIs into a cascading tree structure, then ranks each driver by its sensitivity impact — showing which levers most affect EBITDA and runway at a ±5% change.

**What it covers:**
- KPI Decomposition Tree — visual cascade from Runway (root) through EBITDA → Gross Profit + OpEx → Revenue + COGS → Price + Volume, with each node showing value and health status
- Driver Sensitivity (Tornado Chart) — EBITDA impact of a ±5% change in each driver, ranked from highest to lowest
- Top Lever callout — single most impactful driver identified with dollar impact
- Sensitivity Table — ranked driver list with base value, +5% EBITDA change, -5% EBITDA change, and profitability impact flag

**Who it's for:**
CFO or Finance Business Partner who needs to answer: *What is the single highest-leverage thing the business can do right now? Which initiatives move the needle — and which are noise?*

**Why it exists:**
Leadership teams debate priorities without knowing which drivers actually matter. This module removes that ambiguity — it shows the ranked list of levers with dollar impact attached, so the conversation starts from the math rather than opinion.

**Real-life scenario:**
> Leadership has five initiatives on the table. Everyone has a favorite. The CFO needs to cut three. This module shows that Price has a $18K/mo EBITDA impact at ±5% — 3× bigger than the next lever. The decision gets easier.

### 🔵 Working Capital FP&A
**File:** `working-capital.html`

**What it is:**
A working capital model that connects collections, payables, and inventory timing to cash conversion cycle, short-term cash position, and 12-month cash balance — explaining the gap between P&L profitability and actual cash health.

**What it covers:**
- Cash Conversion Cycle Breakdown — DSO, DIO, DPO, and Net CCC with benchmark comparison and health flags
- 13-Week Cash Flow View — weekly cash in/out/balance for near-term liquidity monitoring
- 12-Month Cash Balance Forecast — rolling cash balance across Base / Upside / Downside with driver assumptions
- Monthly Detail Table — starting cash, revenue, COGS, gross profit, OpEx, net cash flow, and ending cash by month

**Who it's for:**
CFO, Controller, or Finance Business Partner who needs to know: *Why is cash tight if the P&L looks fine? Where is the CCC longest — AR, inventory, or payables? What does cash look like in 13 weeks?*

**Why it exists:**
The P&L and the bank account tell different stories. Working capital management is where the gap lives. This module makes that gap visible and actionable.

**Real-life scenario:**
> Business is growing and profitable, but cash keeps dipping around the same week every month. Collections are running 45 days. Payables are net 30. The mismatch is creating a recurring 15-day cash trough. This module shows it, quantifies it, and models what happens if DSO improves by 10 days.

### 🔵 Capex & Financing Decisions
**File:** `capex-financing.html`

**What it is:**
A capital decision model for evaluating asset acquisition options — comparing the NPV cost, cash impact, monthly payment, and depreciation implications of paying cash, taking a loan, or leasing.

**What it covers:**
- Three-option comparison — Pay Cash vs. Loan vs. Lease with NPV, monthly payment, and total cost for each
- Cash Balance Impact Over Time — line chart showing how each option affects cash balance across the full financing period
- Monthly Cash Outflow Comparison — bar chart showing recurring payment difference between options
- Straight-Line Depreciation Schedule — year-by-year depreciation table for the asset

**Who it's for:**
CFO or Finance Business Partner who needs to answer: *What is the cheapest way to acquire this asset? How does each option affect our cash runway? What is the depreciation impact on the P&L?*

## UX decisions worth noting

- **Decision boxes lead every module** — each module opens with a bold verdict (Positive / Warning / Negative / Recommend) stating a concrete action, not just a summary. The analysis exists to support the decision, not replace it.
- **Board View KPI strip** — every module has a Board View section that surfaces the 4–6 metrics a VP or CFO actually looks at first, before any charts or tables.
- **No scroll jumps on data changes** — all data-trigger buttons (Use Sample, Randomize, Reset, scenario tabs) use a `withStableScroll()` helper that captures and restores viewport position around chart redraws. No content flicker, no viewport snap.
- **Problem-first navigation** — the landing page leads with CFO-level pain statements, not module names. Pick the problem → open the model.
- **Randomize generates coherent scenarios** — not pure noise. Operations Spend, for example, randomly picks a Positive / Mid / Negative outcome mode and generates data consistent with that mode end-to-end.

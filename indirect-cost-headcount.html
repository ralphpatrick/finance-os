    Runway Impact Scenarios

    Runway Impact ?
    💡 So What — Runway Impact
    • ›Every hire shortens runway. This chart shows exactly how many months you trade away under each hiring strategy — making the hire/delay tradeoff explicit and board-ready.
    • ›Conservative hiring preserves 3–6 additional months of runway vs aggressive — enough time to close a funding round or reach a revenue milestone that reduces cash dependence.
    • ›If runway gap between strategies exceeds 4 months: Default to conservative hiring plan and flag aggressive scenario as requiring a capital raise or revenue acceleration commitment.
    • ›If runway < 12 months on either path: Pause all non-critical hires, model cash-neutral staffing (backfill only), and present funding options to the board within 30 days.

    1
    Load Sample Data
    Click Sample in the sidebar. Loads a realistic 12-month dataset: 4 functions, 73–95 employees, $2.4M cash, $185K/mo burn. All charts populate instantly.
    2
    Read the Decision Box
    The top card answers: hire, hold, cut, or raise? Walk through how the rule fires: utilization vs threshold, runway vs floor. Confidence shows proximity to the boundary.
    3
    Show the Coverage Gap
    Scroll to Recommended Hiring Mix. Point out the bottleneck (green highlight). Show full plan vs feasible plan — that gap is exactly what the runway floor prevents. This is the CoE core value-add.
    4
    Run the Knapsack Optimizer
    Click ⚡ Optimize Coverage. The bounded DP knapsack finds the globally optimal allocation — not greedy order. Show the function cards with post-hire utilization. Toggle Show Proof to expose the exact math: budget units, DP dimensions, traceback result.
    5
    Drag the Target Utilization Slider
    Open Assumptions. Drag target util from 85% to 75% — watch the coverage gap, KPIs, and story update live. Demonstrates full model reactivity.
    6
    Show Workforce Economics
    Scroll to Workforce Economics. Revenue per employee is the #1 benchmark investors ask about. Attrition cost surfaces hidden P&L exposure. FTE vs Contractor gives a breakeven utilization number for make-vs-buy decisions.
    7
    Budget vs. Actuals
    Show the 6-month variance table. Persistent over-budget headcount + high utilization = the hiring plan was too conservative. Teams are absorbing demand the model didn’t anticipate. This connects the dots for the CFO.
    8
    Switch Scenarios
    Use Upside / Downside tabs in the sidebar. Show how the hiring decision flips under demand surge vs contraction. This is the risk/sensitivity slide in any board deck — shown live, not in a static table.
    Key talking point: Every number flows from the same dataset. Change one assumption and the decision, story, hiring mix, workforce economics, budget variance, and comp curve all update together. That is what a real Center of Excellence looks like — not a deck refreshed once a quarter.
